## Overview

Donkeykonqueror is a Red Chromatic Dragonborn fighter of considerable size, unconquerable optimism, and a name that has caused administrative problems at wedding registries across three continents. He is on his one thousandth marriage. He states this with the calm authority of someone reciting a perfectly ordinary biographical detail, because to him, it is.

He is not a fool. He is something considerably more interesting: a man of genuine warmth and genuine depth who has, through no particular fault of his own, never quite worked out how to let either of those things show.

> _"I have loved one thousand people. I know a great deal about weddings. I know considerably less about what comes after."_

---

## Backstory

### The Name

The name Donkeykonqueror was given at birth by parents whose naming philosophy historians have described as "ambitious." It has not, in practice, conquered anything. It has, however, caused a significant number of wedding officiants to read it twice, which he has come to regard as a feature.

### Origins

Donkeykonqueror grew up in a Dragonborn clan of warriors — a culture that prized physical excellence, tactical intelligence, and the performance of strength. You showed what you were through what you did. You did not say how you felt. You demonstrated it — in combat, in loyalty, in the keeping of oaths.

This produced a Dragonborn who is genuinely excellent at keeping oaths, and genuinely uncertain what the oath is supposed to feel like from the inside.

### The Marriages — A Partial Record

The first marriage happened at nineteen. It lasted fourteen months and ended in mutual bafflement. His spouse felt she did not know him. He did not know what she meant.

Marriages two through nine hundred and ninety-nine followed variations of this pattern, across a remarkable diversity of species, professions, and geographic locations. Each time, Donkeykonqueror committed fully — to the ceremony, the oath, and the practical logistics of shared life. Each time, something in the gap between the vows and the first anniversary failed to fully connect.

By marriage two hundred, he had begun to suspect the problem was structural.

By marriage five hundred, he had developed strong opinions about florists.

By marriage one thousand, he has achieved a calm expertise about the wedding industrial complex that is genuinely impressive — combined with a quietly held suspicion, not yet examined, that what he keeps reaching for in the ceremony is something the ceremony alone cannot provide.

He is currently managing the logistics of his one thousandth marriage. He is not currently sure how it is going.

## Character

Donkeykonqueror is affable, confident, and largely unbothered by the fact that his personal life is, by most external metrics, a sequence of impressive disasters. He discusses his marriages with the tone of someone reviewing military campaigns — analytically, with occasional pride, and without excessive self-flagellation.

He is genuinely warm, in the way of someone who has warmth in abundance and no reliable delivery mechanism for it. It comes out sideways — in actions rather than words, in showing up rather than announcing he will, in the specific loyalty of someone who means every oath he swears even when he cannot explain why.

He is also, without trying, frequently very funny — through a combination of literal-mindedness, enormous physical presence, and a relationship with social convention that is respectful in theory and occasionally catastrophic in practice.

---

## Relationship With The Party

Donkeykonqueror appears and disappears with the rhythm of a man whose personal life is administratively complex. He does not explain his absences in detail. The party has learned not to ask.

What is worth noting — and what Donkeykonqueror himself has not yet fully noted — is that he always comes back. Through whatever matrimonial, legal, or ceremonial chaos is currently consuming his schedule, he finds his way back to these specific people. For a man whose defining characteristic is the difficulty of making things stick, his consistency with the party is the most honest thing about him.

He knows this, somewhere. He has not yet found the words for it.

---

## Motivation

Donkeykonqueror would tell you his motivation is straightforward: fight well, honour his oaths, and find out whether marriage one thousand is the one that finally makes sense. This is all true. It is also, increasingly, not the whole story.

The whole story involves a group of people he keeps returning to, for reasons he has not examined, in a world that keeps requiring him to be present in ways that go beyond combat effectiveness. He is, against his prior experience, beginning to find this interesting rather than confusing.

He would not say this out loud. He is working on it.
