## Overview

Zaptos is a Wood Elf wizard who joined a party of adventurers because he needed a job and this was the first opportunity that did not require references. He is, by most observable metrics, unhinged — not in the dangerous sense, but in the way of a man whose internal compass spins freely and has never quite settled on north. He is enthusiastic, unpredictable, occasionally brilliant, frequently chaotic, and absolutely, pathologically committed to his Ray of Frost cantrip in situations where it is anywhere from unnecessary to catastrophic.

He does not take most things seriously. He would tell you this is simply his personality. He would be mostly right.

> _"I'm not lost. I'm just… taking a very scenic route to wherever I'm going."_

## Backstory

### The Prodigy Nobody Supervised

Zaptos grew up in a Wood Elf community that valued attunement to nature, careful cultivation of the self, and measured, intentional growth — all things he found deeply reasonable in theory and structurally incompatible with his personal temperament in practice. He was not a bad student. He was a spectacularly distracted one, possessed of a genuine gift for magical theory that his teachers found both impressive and exhausting in roughly equal measure.

He discovered Ray of Frost at an age when most young elves were still learning to feel the shape of magic. He used it immediately on a classmate's lunch. This was considered antisocial. He considered it exploratory. The disagreement was never fully resolved.

His magical education was characterised by the same pattern repeated across different subjects: rapid, intuitive grasp of the underlying principle; creative and entirely unsanctioned application of said principle; baffled response from supervising adults; Zaptos genuinely not understanding what he had done wrong.

### The Graduation That Launched A Thousand Odd Jobs

Zaptos graduated with marks that reflected exactly the following: a student of significant raw talent, no discernible specialisation, and a personal relationship with academic convention best described as _theoretical._

What followed was everything, in no particular order. He tutored minor nobles' children — and was asked to leave after demonstrating Ray of Frost as a classroom management technique. He apprenticed briefly with an enchanter who specialised in warmth-retention items — the irony of which he did not register until much later. He attempted to establish himself as a magical consultant for a merchants' guild — and was dismissed after accidentally freezing a shipment of perishables that were not meant to be preserved in that manner or to that degree. He scouted terrain for a cartographer — until he scouted the wrong terrain.

At no point during any of this did Zaptos experience what he would describe as a calling. He experienced employment, termination, and the occasional moment of genuine satisfaction quickly followed by something going wrong.

### The Job Posting

The posting was not, technically, a job posting. It was closer to a vague word-of-mouth suggestion that a group of adventurers might be willing to take on another member if that member had useful skills and showed up. Zaptos had useful skills. He showed up.

He has not yet asked what the party is ultimately trying to accomplish. He has been meaning to.

## The Ray of Frost

Zaptos' signature cantrip is, at this point, less a spell and more a worldview. He knows its range to the inch. He can predict its spread across various surface types. He has used it in approximately four hundred situations for which it was not designed. He does not often reach for his other spells first. Ray of Frost is his tool — the one thing that has never let him down — and he sees no compelling reason to change a system that is, by his own assessment, working fine.

Others may disagree. They are welcome to do so from a safe distance.

## Family

**Elara Zaptos** _(Mother)_ — A Wood Elf herbalist of patient temperament and formidable organisational skill, both of which skipped a generation. She sends letters. She includes practical advice and pressed herbs she thinks might be calming. He uses the herbs as bookmarks. The letters he keeps.

**Maerun Zaptos** _(Father)_ — A quiet forest warden who noticed, earlier than anyone else, that his son's distractibility was not laziness but a form of attention that simply moved differently. He said so once, carefully. Zaptos changed the subject. It has probably been sitting in the back of his mind since.

## Character & Motivation

Zaptos joined the party for work. He has stayed because it turns out the work is more interesting than most things he has tried, and the people are stranger than most people he has met, which he means as a compliment.

He is not searching for anything in particular. He is not running from anything he would admit to. He is, as he would put it, simply seeing where the road goes — which is true, and also possibly not the whole story.

---

## What People Notice About Zaptos

He is funnier than he is organised. He is more capable than he presents. He reaches for Ray of Frost in situations that call for something else and occasionally, inexplicably, it works. He pays attention in the way of someone who has learned that looking like you are not paying attention is its own kind of advantage.

He does not talk about what he wants. Not because he is evasive — he is many things, but not evasive — but because he has not entirely worked it out yet. He is, by his own account, taking the scenic route.

He will get there.
