## Overview

Euler is a wizard who does not belong here — not in this era, not in this city, and not in this particular tavern arguing about the price of a room he intends to leave without paying. He is sharp, self-contained, and operating at all times with the specific efficiency of a man who has decided that the most important person in any room is himself. He is not cruel for cruelty's sake. He is simply, comprehensively, oriented toward his own survival and his own restoration — every alliance, every calculation, every act of apparent cooperation pointing back toward the one goal that matters: figuring out what happened to him, and finding his way back.

He is also aware, in the way of very intelligent people who have been alone with their own thoughts for too long, that something is missing. He cannot name it precisely. He has not yet tried very hard.

## Backstory

### The Exile

Euler arrived in the current era without choosing to. One moment he was — somewhere else, somewhen else — and the next he was here, in a world centuries behind the one he came from, with fragments where his memories should be and a methodology that does not quite fit the magical landscape around him.

What he knows: he was banished. By whom, for what, and from precisely when, he cannot fully access. What he can access is the shape of the loss — power he used to have and cannot reach, knowledge that surfaces in glimpses and then retreats, a symbol he draws without knowing why.

What he does not know: whether what he did deserved this. Whether the people who sent him here were right. Whether the version of himself that existed before the exile is someone he would recognise.

He is working on finding out. In the meantime, he works on surviving.

### The Sigil

A circle, nested within a triangle, crossed by a single horizontal line. He has drawn it on the corners of maps, the margins of spellbooks, the condensation on tavern windows. He does not know what it means. He knows it matters. He has never seen it in any text or tradition of this era, which tells him something about where — and when — it comes from.

### The Spellcasting

Euler's magic is recognisably wizardly but with anomalies that he has stopped trying to explain to other practitioners. Effects arrive at slightly wrong angles. His approach to magical systems reflects an intuition for underlying structure that goes beyond what any training in this era should produce. He describes spells in terminology no one around him recognises. He occasionally reaches for tools that are not there.

He calls this a side effect of the exile. He is not wrong.

## Character

Euler is shrewd, calculative, and faintly superior — not theatrically, but in the specific way of someone who has concluded, based on available evidence, that he is operating several steps ahead of most rooms he enters. He is not always wrong about this. He is frequently wrong about what being several steps ahead is actually for.

He is capable of warmth when warmth is tactically useful. He is capable of generosity when generosity produces a return. He is not yet particularly practised at either when they cost him something with no calculable benefit.

He is, however, observant enough to notice the people around him more accurately than his stated self-interest would suggest. He tracks their patterns. He anticipates their needs — usually so he can account for them as variables, but the tracking is there. Something is paying attention that has not quite admitted what it is paying attention to.

---

## Motivation

Euler's stated motivation is clear: survive, recover what was taken, determine the nature of his exile, and find a way back to his own time. Every decision is in service of this. Every alliance is provisional. Every attachment is a liability he is managing.

This is what he tells himself. It is mostly true. It is also, increasingly, not the whole story — though he would dispute this if asked, and would be convincing about it, and would think about it afterward in the dark.

---

## What People Notice About Euler

He is the smartest person in most rooms and wants you to know it without having to say it. He solves problems with an efficiency that is occasionally alarming and frequently useful. He does not explain himself, his methods, or the occasional anachronistic detail that surfaces in his spellwork.

He also, for someone who claims to operate on pure self-interest, shows up. He is there when things go badly. He contributes when contribution is costly. He would tell you this is tactical. The people around him are beginning to have questions about that explanation.

> _"I came here to find my way back. I did not expect this to be complicated."_
