## Overview

Pollinates is a Dhampir of 2,000 years — old enough to have watched empires rise and crumble, forests regrow over battlefields, and the names of gods fall out of fashion. He carries that age not with arrogance, but with a quiet, weathered sadness, like a tree that has survived too many storms to be surprised by thunder. He is the youngest surviving member of the Brassavola Nodosa Clan, an orphan twice over — first by fire, then by time.

---

## Origins — The County of Nodosa, Lands of Brassavola

The Brassavola Nodosa Clan were a noble bloodline of the County of Nodosa, a misty, forested region in the lands of Brassavola — a place where the boundary between the natural world and older, stranger things was always thin. The Clan were not conquerors or kings. They were keepers — of old groves, of bloodline traditions, and of a careful peace between the mortal and the nocturnal.

Pollinates' mother was a human Druid of some renown, a woman who moved through the forest as though the trees leaned aside to let her pass. His father was a pureblood vampire — ancient, composed, and deeply private — who had long since chosen stillness over hunger. By all accounts, theirs was not a love story the world was willing to accept. But within the walls of Nodosa, it existed.

Pollinates was born the youngest of the Clan, smaller and quieter than his kin, already half-caught between two natures he did not yet understand.

He was five years old when the war came.

---

## The Fire — Year 0

The details of the war that consumed Nodosa have blurred over two millennia, as all things do. What Pollinates remembers is not strategy or politics — it is heat. The smell of smoke threading through old wood. His mother's voice, and then the absence of it. His father, who could have survived almost anything, choosing not to leave her side.

Both died in the fire. The Clan scattered or perished with them.

Pollinates survived — small, overlooked, and alone. He does not know who carried him out. He is not sure he wants to.

> _"I have tried, over the centuries, to remember their faces without the firelight in them. I have not yet succeeded."_

---

## The First Era — Wandering (Years 5 – ~400)

The centuries that followed were formless. Without family, without a Clan, Pollinates drifted — through forests, through villages that aged and died while he remained, through the cold company of solitude. He was not yet in command of his Dhampir nature; the blood hunger came weekly like a tide he could not fully explain, and sleep claimed him every few days whether he willed it or not.

It was during this era that his mother's Druidic inheritance began to surface. He found he could sense the mood of a forest the way others sense weather. He began to hear what the land was holding — old grief, slow growth, patient waiting. Nature did not ask him what he was. It simply accepted his presence, and he learned to accept its rhythms in return.

The feather cloak came to him in this period — its precise origins deliberately kept vague even to him. Whether inherited, stumbled upon, or gifted by something that moves through old forests, he does not say. What it gave him was freedom: the ability to dissolve into a raven, or into many ravens, and scatter himself across the sky. For centuries, this was how he processed grief — by becoming something that did not have to remember.

---

## The Second Era — Purpose (~Years 400 – 1200)

Somewhere in his fifth century, the wandering began to feel less like freedom and more like avoidance. Pollinates started attaching himself — briefly, carefully — to causes, groves, communities. He served as a warden of wild places. He intervened in conflicts where the natural world was threatened. He was never a hero in the celebrated sense. He moved quietly, did what needed doing, and left before anyone could grow too fond of him.

This era shaped his Druidic craft into something deliberate rather than instinctive. He learned to use his shapeshifting with precision. He learned when to be many ravens and when to be one. He learned, painfully, that caring about things that do not last is its own kind of wound — one that does not close, but one that can be carried.

He also learned to manage his hunger with discipline rather than denial. The blood-need is part of what he is; fighting it entirely only made the pull worse. A measured, infrequent yield kept him functional, kept him himself.

---

## The Third Era — Withdrawal (~Years 1200 – 1900)

The weight of centuries eventually drove him inward. Too many names forgotten. Too many graves left behind. Pollinates retreated from the world in a long, slow way — not dramatically, but incrementally. He stopped attaching. He stopped intervening. He watched from a distance, in raven form more often than not, and told himself this was wisdom.

Whether it was wisdom or exhaustion is a question he has not resolved.

These centuries left him with a deep, habitual stillness. He is not cold, but he is careful. He does not rush to connect, because connection has always meant eventual loss. He is more comfortable as a raven among ravens than as a person among people.

---

## The Present — Rejoining the World

Something drew Pollinates back. He could not name it precisely — a restlessness, a shift in the land's mood, or perhaps simply the stubborn flicker of something in him that refused to fully go out. He has re-entered the world, attached himself — tentatively, watchfully — to a party of adventurers, and is navigating what it means to be present again after centuries of careful distance.

He is the oldest one in most rooms, and often the quietest. He notices things others miss. He grieves things others have never heard of. And somewhere beneath the stillness, beneath the 2,000 years of practiced detachment, is a five-year-old boy who never quite understood why the fire had to take everyone.

## Nature & Abilities

**Dhampir Physiology** — Born of a pureblood vampire father and a human mother, Pollinates exists between states. He requires blood approximately once a week — not ravenously, but as a steady, rhythmic need he has long since integrated into his routine. Sleep claims him every three days, deeper and less negotiable than mortal rest.

**Druidic Inheritance** — His mother's craft runs through him. He can sense the emotional and spiritual state of natural spaces — old growth, disturbed land, places of power or grief. His command of Druidic magic developed slowly over centuries and is now both instinctive and disciplined.

**The Feather Cloak** — Pollinates can shapeshift into a single raven or fragment himself into a flock of many ravens, using this ability to travel vast distances or simply to be elsewhere for a while. The cloak's true origin remains one of the few mysteries he does not press.

---

## Relationships & Hooks

**The Brassavola Nodosa Clan** — Believed extinct. Whether any bloodline or tradition survived two millennia is unknown — and potentially a significant discovery waiting to be made.

**The Fire & The War** — The conflict that killed his parents has never been fully explained to him. Who started it, who profited from it, and whether anything connected to it still exists in the world are open questions.

**The Feather Cloak** — Where did it truly come from? Does it have loyalties of its own? Does something on the other end of those raven-roads know where Pollinates goes?

**The Party** — For the first time in centuries, Pollinates is choosing to stay somewhere. What that costs him, and what it gives back, is the quietest arc he is living.

## DM Plot Notes

> - Pollinates' age means he may have encountered historical events, factions, or figures that others only know as legend — use sparingly for weight
> - His blood hunger and sleep cycle are vulnerabilities that could be leveraged by enemies aware of his nature
> - The feather cloak can serve as a supernatural mystery thread — something old recognising him, messages arriving via ravens, etc.
> - His emotional arc is re-learning investment; NPCs who get genuinely close to him are high-stakes
> - The Nodosa Clan's extinction is not confirmed — a surviving relic, descendant, or splinter tradition could destabilise his sense of being entirely alone in the world
