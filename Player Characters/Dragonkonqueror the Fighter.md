## Overview

Donkeykonqueror is a Chromatic Dragonborn fighter of considerable physical presence, dubious marital history, and a name that has caused administrative problems at wedding registries across three continents. He is enormous, enthusiastic, intermittently present, and on his one thousandth marriage — a figure he states with the calm authority of someone reciting a tax return, as though the number is unremarkable and the real story is whatever comes next.

He is not a fool. He is not shallow. He is something considerably more interesting and considerably more complicated: a man of genuine depth who has never once successfully communicated that depth to another living person, including himself. He knows things — about honour, about loyalty, about what it means to stay — that he has never managed to say out loud or feel in full. He keeps marrying because somewhere in the ritual of commitment is the thing he is reaching for. He has not yet worked out that the ritual is not the thing.

The party is, without knowing it, the closest he has ever come to finding what he has been looking for. He will not say this. He may not know it yet. It is nonetheless true.

> _"I have loved one thousand people. I know a great deal about weddings. I know considerably less about what comes after."_

## The Gospel Thread — From the Head to the Heart

Donkeykonqueror's wound is the gap between knowing and feeling — between the map and the territory, between the ceremony and the meaning. He is not emotionally avoidant in the way of someone who has decided feelings are dangerous. He is emotionally avoidant in the way of someone who was never given the vocabulary, never shown the model, never had a safe enough space to practise. The feelings are there. They are substantial. They have simply never successfully made it from wherever they live in him to anywhere anyone else can see them — including him.

The scripture that speaks to his arc is not a rebuke. It is a promise:

> _"I will give you a new heart and put a new spirit in you; I will remove from you your heart of stone and give you a heart of flesh."_ — Ezekiel 36:26

Donkeykonqueror does not have a heart of stone. He has a heart that has been locked in a room it does not know how to leave. The gospel movement of his arc is not the acquisition of feelings he does not have — it is the slow, occasionally slapstick, ultimately tender discovery that the feelings were always there, and that the people around him are safe enough to show them to.

## Backstory

### The Name

The name Donkeykonqueror was given at birth by parents whose naming philosophy has been described by historians as "ambitious" and by the local registrar as "a problem for future generations." It has not, in practice, conqueror anything. It has, however, caused a significant number of clerics to read it twice at wedding ceremonies, which Donkeykonqueror has come to regard as a feature rather than a bug.

### Origins

Donkeykonqueror is a Red Chromatic Dragonborn — scaled in deep crimson, possessed of a fire breath that he deploys with the cheerful confidence of someone who has never once considered that fire might be an inappropriate solution to a given problem. He is large even by Dragonborn standards, moves through the world with the gentle obliviousness of a creature who has simply never had to worry about his personal safety, and has left a trail of singed architecture, bewildered in-laws, and legally binding documents across the known world.

He grew up in a Dragonborn clan of warriors — a culture that prized physical excellence, tactical intelligence, and the performance of strength above almost everything else. Emotional expression was not prohibited so much as simply never modelled. You showed what you were through what you did. You did not say how you felt. You demonstrated it — in combat, in loyalty, in the keeping of oaths.

This produced a Dragonborn who is genuinely excellent at keeping oaths and genuinely terrible at knowing what the oath is supposed to feel like from the inside.

### The Marriages — A Brief Institutional History

The first marriage happened at nineteen. She was a half-elf cartographer with excellent spatial reasoning and a low tolerance for fire-related property damage. The marriage lasted fourteen months. It ended not in animosity but in a kind of mutual bafflement — she felt she did not know him; he did not know what she meant.

The second through the forty-seventh followed a similar pattern across different configurations of species, profession, and geographic location. Each time, Donkeykonqueror committed fully to the ceremony, the oath, and the practical logistics of shared life. Each time, somewhere between the vows and the first anniversary, something failed to cross the gap between his interior and the marriage. His spouses consistently reported that he was kind, reliable, occasionally on fire, and somehow absent — present in every room and unreachable in all of them.

By the two hundredth marriage, he had begun to suspect the problem was structural rather than situational.

By the five hundredth, he had developed strong opinions about florists.

By the one thousandth, he has achieved a kind of calm expertise about the wedding industrial complex that is frankly impressive, combined with a privately held suspicion — not yet articulated, not yet examined — that what he has been looking for in the institution of marriage is something the institution alone cannot provide.

He is currently between marriages. He is not currently between engagements.

> **DM Note:** The marriages are comedy, but they are also the mechanism of his wound — each one an attempt to feel the thing he knows he should feel, performed with full commitment, missing the target in a way he cannot diagnose. Do not let the joke flatten the pathos. Both can be true simultaneously, and the best moments in his arc will be when the comedy and the genuine tenderness occupy the same breath.

## Character & Presentation

Donkeykonqueror presents as confident, affable, and largely unbothered by the fact that his life is, by most external metrics, a sequence of impressive disasters. He is not defensive about the marriages. He discusses them with the same tone he would use to discuss military campaigns — analytically, with occasional pride in specific tactical decisions, and a willingness to note where things went wrong without excessive self-flagellation.

He is genuinely warm, in the way of someone who has warmth in abundance and no reliable delivery mechanism for it. It comes out sideways — in actions rather than words, in showing up rather than saying he will, in the specific loyalty of someone who has sworn oaths professionally for a century and means every one of them even when he cannot explain why.

He is also, without meaning to be, frequently very funny — through a combination of literal-mindedness, physical scale, and a relationship with social convention that is respectful in theory and chaotic in practice.

## Relationship With The Party

Donkeykonqueror appears and disappears from the party's narrative with the specific rhythm of a man whose personal life is administratively complex. He does not explain his absences in detail. The party has learned not to ask, partly because the answers are always marriage-related and partly because the answers are always longer than expected.

What the party may not fully appreciate is that he comes back. Every time. Without fail. Through whatever logistical, legal, or ceremonial chaos is currently consuming his personal life, he finds his way back to these specific people. For a man whose defining characteristic is the inability to make relationships stick, his consistency with the party is worth noting.

He has not noted it yet. He will.

> **DM Note:** His disappearances and returns are the comedic surface of a deeply significant arc truth — he keeps leaving and he keeps coming back, and the party is the first context in his life where the coming back has become reflexive. When he finally understands that, it will be one of the most genuine moments his player has access to.

## Key Figures

#### Spouse #1000 _(currently ongoing, name TBD)_

Currently the reigning holder of the title. The details of this marriage are appropriately chaotic — the circumstances of how it began are unclear even to Donkeykonqueror, and the circumstances of how it will end are currently being negotiated by at least two lawyers and a notary who has seen things. This spouse should be introduced as a comedic presence before becoming, unexpectedly, the moment where Donkeykonqueror finally attempts to say something true about how he feels — and discovers, to his own shock, that he can.

#### The Registrar of Marriages, City of Neverwinter _(recurring NPC)_

A gnome bureaucrat who has personally processed forty-seven of Donkeykonqueror's marriage certificates and has developed a thousand-yard stare specifically for when he walks through the door. He is not a villain. He is a man doing his job under extraordinary circumstances. He keeps a running file. It is the thickest file in the office. He has named it simply: _Him._

#### A Former Spouse Who Stayed Friends _(to be introduced)_

Not all one thousand ended badly. One of them — spouse number perhaps two hundred and something — became, improbably, a genuine friend. They know him better than almost anyone. They will appear at a dramatically appropriate moment and say something honest about what they saw in him that he never showed anyone else. This is the moment Donkeykonqueror learns that he was known — imperfectly, incompletely, but genuinely known — and that someone chose to stay in his life anyway, even without the ceremony. The friendship is the thing. It was always the thing.

## Character Arc — Phase by Phase

### Phase 1 — Performing Commitment

Donkeykonqueror shows up, fights well, swears oaths with impressive conviction, and disappears periodically to manage the latest matrimonial development. He is excellent at the form of loyalty and affection. He is not yet practised at the substance of it.

**Trigger events:**

- A party member asks him, sincerely, why he keeps getting married. He gives a technically accurate answer that says nothing
- He does something genuinely caring for a party member — practically, concretely, without announcement — and when thanked, deflects immediately
- He returns from an absence and something in the party has shifted while he was gone. He notices. He says nothing. He pays closer attention.

### Phase 2 — The Gap Becomes Visible

The party encounters something that requires not action but presence — someone in grief, someone in crisis, a moment that calls for emotional attunement rather than combat effectiveness. Donkeykonqueror stands at the edge of this moment, knows the right thing, and cannot make it cross the gap from knowing to doing. He does something practical instead. It helps, a little. It is not the thing.

**Key tension:** He knows what the moment requires. He has read about grief, about comfort, about what people need in hard times. The knowledge is accurate and entirely insufficient. He stands in the gap between the knowing and the doing and does not know how to cross it.

**Key moment — played for comedy and pathos simultaneously:** He attempts to express something emotional to a party member and produces something technically correct, completely wrong in tone, and accidentally very funny. The party member — if they are paying attention — sees through the comedy to the genuine thing underneath it and responds to that instead of to the surface. Donkeykonqueror does not know what to do with being seen accurately. He is briefly very still.

### Phase 3 — The Former Spouse Appears

The friend from marriage number two-hundred-and-something arrives — perhaps in Phandalin, perhaps in Wave Echo Cave, perhaps at the most inconvenient possible moment. They know him. They say something true about him in front of the party. Something he did not know they had noticed. Something he has never said about himself.

**Gospel moment:** He was known. Imperfectly, yes — but genuinely. Someone looked at the performing, oath-swearing, practically-affectionate, emotionally-avoidant Dragonborn and saw a person of real warmth and real depth, and chose to remain in his life in whatever form was available. This is grace at the comedic register: not a dramatic revelation but a quiet, slightly embarrassing, entirely real moment of being loved as-is.

**Key question for the player:** _What if the thing you have been performing for a thousand marriages is something you actually have — and the people in front of you can already see it, even when you can't?_

### Phase 4 — Learning to Say It

Donkeykonqueror begins, with visible effort and intermittent success, to let what is inside him cross the gap. Not fluently. Not gracefully. With the specific awkwardness of someone learning a physical skill in public — occasionally getting it right, occasionally getting it spectacularly wrong, never quite giving up.

**Key moment:** He says something true to a party member. Not a declaration, not a speech — something small and direct and genuine. He probably immediately says something ridiculous afterward to cover it. The party member receives both the genuine thing and the covering thing, and says nothing, and it is the most affirming response he has ever received.

**Gospel resonance:** The heart of flesh is not given all at once. It grows incrementally, in the specific warmth of being received without conditions. Donkeykonqueror is being given, by these people, what no marriage ceremony ever provided: the experience of being known over time, in ordinary moments, without requiring the performance to be perfect.

### Phase 5 — Staying

Donkeykonqueror's final form is not a man who has stopped being chaotic, or stopped getting married, or stopped being intermittently on fire. It is a man who has learned to stay — not in the ceremonial sense, but in the actual sense. Who has discovered that the belonging he has been formalising in legal documents for a century was available all along in the specific, un-ceremonied reality of these people.

He still disappears sometimes. He always comes back. But now, when he comes back, he says so — in whatever awkward, oversized, slightly scorched way he can manage.

> _"I have had one thousand weddings. This—" (gestures at the party, at the road, at the general situation) "—is the first thing that has felt like a marriage."_

## Limit Break

### Nature: The Oath That Means It

Donkeykonqueror has sworn one thousand oaths. He has meant every one of them, in the specific way of someone who is very good at the form of commitment and still learning the substance. His limit break is the moment those two things finally, completely align — when he swears something, or acts on something sworn, with his whole self behind it rather than just his very considerable body and impressive legal documentation.

### Trigger Conditions

**Condition One — A Party Member Is Being Abandoned** Not merely in danger — specifically being left, discarded, or treated as though they are not worth staying for. This is the precise trigger because it is the thing Donkeykonqueror understands most intimately from the inside, even if he has never named it. He has been on both sides of the leaving. He knows what it costs. The sight of it happening to someone he has claimed — without ceremony, without documentation, but genuinely claimed — produces a response that bypasses calculation entirely.

**Condition Two — No One Else Is Moving** The wound condition. The limit break fires specifically when the moment requires someone to stay or act and everyone else is frozen, overwhelmed, or otherwise unable. Donkeykonqueror, who has spent his life performing commitment in rooms full of witnesses, discovers that the truest version of commitment happens when no one is watching and there is no ceremony to hold it together. Just him, and the choice, and the person who needs someone to stay.

### What It Looks Like

He gets very quiet first. This is alarming. Donkeykonqueror is not a quiet presence. When the noise stops, something is happening.

Then he moves — not with the cheerful, slightly-chaotic energy of his standard combat approach, but with something slower and more absolute. His fire breath, when it comes, is not the exuberant burst of normal operation. It is deliberate and total, the specific output of a creature who has stopped performing and started meaning it. Enemies who have been treating him as a comedic obstacle reassess this position rapidly.

In the aftermath, he will say something to the party member he acted for. It will be short. It will be direct. It will be the most emotionally legible sentence he has produced in a hundred years of trying. It will probably be followed immediately by something about needing to reschedule a divorce hearing.

The comedy and the genuine thing, one more time, in the same breath.

### Gospel Dimension

The limit break is the moment Donkeykonqueror's heart of flesh shows up in public without being asked. He did not plan it. He did not perform it. He simply, finally, meant it — and meaning it came out in a way that anyone watching could see and feel. For a man who has spent a century knowing the right things without being able to feel them, this is the gospel moment: not acquired by effort, but given — in the specific warmth of having people worth staying for, and discovering that staying, when it is real, feels like himself.

## DM Notes

> - **The comedy is the delivery mechanism for the tenderness** — never sacrifice one for the other. The funniest moments in his arc should also be the most human
> - **His absences are narratively significant** — track what the party does while he is gone and make sure he notices something specific when he returns. This builds the arc of his attentiveness without requiring him to be present
> - **The Registrar of Neverwinter** is a gift — use him as a recurring cameo, always in the most inappropriate possible moment, always with the file
> - **The former spouse should be warm, not bitter** — their function is grace, not closure. They are the evidence that he was always worth knowing
> - **His limit break line** — the one short, direct, genuine thing he says — should be prepared in advance. Give it weight by contrast with everything preceding it. Keep it simple. _"You matter to me."_ Three words from a man who has written a thousand vows is worth more than the thousand vows.
> - **The player's personal context** — homeschooled, head knowledge without heart knowledge, struggles with expressing emotion — means this arc is doing real pastoral work. The character's journey from knowing to feeling is an invitation for the player to practise the same thing in a safe, comedic, low-stakes register. Treat it with care and with joy. Both are required.
