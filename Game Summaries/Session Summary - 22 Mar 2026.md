## Quests Received

Before setting out, the party took stock of several outstanding leads: a request to find Reidoth the Druid in Thundertree, the recovery of Mirna's family heirloom hidden somewhere in the ruins, the clearing of undead reported at Old Owl Well, an orc threat at Wyvern Tor, and the ever-looming main quest of locating Cragmaw Castle. With multiple threads before them, the party resolved to tackle Thundertree first, hoping to address two quests in a single expedition.

---

## Into the Ruins

The party arrived at the blighted town of Thundertree to find it every bit as forsaken as rumour had suggested. The outermost abandoned houses proved to be anything but empty — twig blights lurked within, and the party's first steps into town were met with hostility from the twisted plant creatures. They dispatched them and pressed deeper into the ruins.

Investigating the old bar, the party disturbed a nest of ash zombies — the remnants of those who perished when the volcanic ash swept through the town long ago. After a grim but successful fight, the bar was cleared and the party moved on.

---

## Reidoth's Warning

The party eventually located Reidoth the Druid, very much alive and deeply familiar with Thundertree's dangers. He proved a valuable source of intelligence, warning the party of two significant threats still active in the ruins: a group of suspicious figures clad in black masks and cloaks who had taken an interest in the town, and more pressingly, a dragon that had made its lair in the old tower. Reidoth made clear that the creature was not to be trifled with lightly.

---

## The Barn Ambush

While the party was deep in conversation with Reidoth, Pollinates broke away to investigate a nearby barn — and walked straight into a trap. Twig blights were lying in wait, and in moments Pollinates found themselves severely outnumbered and in serious danger. The alarm was raised and the party rushed to respond, but the situation rapidly deteriorated. Wave after wave of twig blights converged on the party's position — three successive surges threatening to overwhelm them entirely.

Through desperate fighting and no small amount of resolve, the party held the line. The ambush was beaten back, and the last of the converging blights were cut down. It was a closer call than anyone would have liked.

---

## Rest

Battered and drained, the party withdrew to Reidoth's reinforced watchtower dwelling — the safest structure in the ruins — and settled in for a long rest, grateful for solid walls and a druid who knew how to hold a position. Thundertree's remaining dangers, including the masked strangers, the dragon, and Mirna's heirloom, still await them come morning.
